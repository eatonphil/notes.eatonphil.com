# Writing to be read ## May 18, 2018 There is a daily fight in the writing and maintenance of documentation, checklists, emails, guides, etc. Each provides immense value; a document may be the key to an important process. The goal is to remove barriers -- to encourage understanding and correct application of what has been noted -- without requiring a change in the character of the reader. Ignoring imperfections in the *ideas* transcribed into writing, there are particular aesthetic approaches I take to (hopefully) make my notes more effective. These ideas have been influenced by readings on writing, pyschology, and user experience. I'd immediately point to [On Writing Well](https://amzn.to/2rT0dsE), [Thinking Fast and Slow](https://amzn.to/2IttNAl), and [Nielsen Norman](https://www.nngroup.com/) research. ### Language correctness The low-hanging fruit is spelling and grammatical correctness. It is not difficult to provide. However don't be a grammar stickler unreasonably, language is flexible and always changing. Use full sentences, capitalize appropriately, and use punctuation. Don't allow anyone the opportunity to take your work less seriously by screwing up the basics. ### Structuring sentences and paragraphs Keep your sentences short. And avoid run-on sentences; they are always difficult to parse. If you use more than two commas in a sentences (aside from in lists), the sentence is terrible. Split it up. In some cases your sentence may even be perfectly correct without certain commas. If you ever have a list containing another list, separate the outer list with semi-colons instead of commas to differentiate. Keep your paragraphs short too. The longer sentences and paragraphs go, the more intimidating they look and will discourage readers from continuing. #### Visually speaking Make your line height [120-145%](https://practicaltypography.com/line-spacing.html) the height of the font. Increasing the spacing between lines will make the paragraph look less dense. Additionally, a number of studies suggest that limiting the width of paragraphs of text increases readability. For best results, limit the width such that [50-75 characters](https://baymard.com/blog/line-length-readability) appear per line of text. #### Don't put checklists in paragraphs If a document describes concrete steps that should be followed and can be reasonably summarized, don't mask them within paragraphs of text. Instead use an ordered or unordered list to clearly enumerate the expectations. You cannot expect a checklist to be followed when it is hidden unclearly within the sentences of a paragraph. ### Structuring sections Always provide headers (with an HTML `id` attribute) to give relief and to allow a direct link to a particular section in a long page. If the page has more than two sections or vertically flows beyond a single screen, consider adding a table of contents at the top of the page to allow the reader to find the exact section she needs.
