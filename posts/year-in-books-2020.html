# The year in books: 20 to recommend in 2020 ## December 27, 2020 ###### books,reading This year I finished 47 books, up from last year but not a personal best. The breakdown was 17 non-fiction and 30 fiction. Another 20-30 remain started but unfinished this year. ### Non-fiction The 8 non-fiction books I most recommend are: * [Fashionapolis: The Price of Fast Fashion and the Future of Clothes](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/51034048-fashionopolis) (Must read) * [Effective Python: 90 Specific Ways to Write Better Python](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/48566725-effective-python) (Must read; truly excellent for Python programmers, I recommend this to anyone I work with) * [The Machine that Changed the World](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/93904.The_Machine_That_Changed_the_World) (Must read) * [Europe: The Struggle for Supremacy from 1453 to the Present](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/16043511-europe) * [Wind, Sand and Stars](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/19606799-wind-sand-and-stars) * [American Colussus: The Triumph of Capitalism, 1865-1900](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/11169043-american-colossus) * [Making Common Sense of Japan](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/2360599.Making_Common_Sense_of_Japan) * [The German Genius](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/8155672-the-german-genius) The 3 books I recommend you not to waste time on are: "The Two Koreas", "The Price of Inequality", and "Ninety Percent of Everything: Inside Shipping". #### The whole list * [The Two Koreas by Don Oberdorfer](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/235560.The_Two_Koreas) * Interesting but not a huge fan, seemed pretty biased against South Korea somehow * [Forbidden Nation: A History of Taiwan by Jonathan Manthorpe](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/88546.Forbidden_Nation) * [Effective Python: 90 Specific Ways to Write Better Python by Brett Slatkin](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/48566725-effective-python) * [A Philosophy of Software Design by John Ousterhout](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/43701534-a-philosophy-of-software-design) * Came as a recommendation from someone on Twitter, ultimately not a huge fan.
